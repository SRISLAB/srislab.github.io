SRIS Laboratory

Smart, Reliable, and Interpretable Systems — we integrate logic, physics, and learning to engineer trustworthy, high-performance systems for intelligent manufacturing and critical infrastructure.

Our unified methodology—combining Signal Temporal Logic (STL), causal inference, and physics-guided priors—drives interpretable diagnosis, STL-driven safe autonomy, verifiable machine learning, and fleet‑level health management.

Research Thrusts

Our work is organized around three interconnected research thrusts, each addressing fundamental challenges in building trustworthy intelligent systems.

Neuro‑Symbolic Foundation Models

STL-regularized pretraining, causal knowledge graphs, and rule distillation for audit-ready industrial AI that produces machine-checkable rationales.

Causal‑Logic PHM

Physics-guided features + causal graphs + STL abstractions to reconstruct fault propagation chains and recommend cost-aware interventions across fleets.

STL‑Driven Safe Autonomy

STL contracts, reference governors, and residual RL for dual-arm & mobile manipulation that is safe by construction and fast by design.
